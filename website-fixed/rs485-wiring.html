Termination Resistors

RS-485 needs 120Î© termination resistors across the data lines at both physical ends of the bus to prevent signal reflections.

  • Many devices have a built-in termination resistor with a DIP switch or jumper â€” check the manual
  • Only enable termination on the first and last devices on the bus
  • Never put termination in the middle of the bus
  • For short runs (<50m) at slow baud rates (9600), you can often get away without termination â€” but always install it for production

Grounding

Despite RS-485 being called "2-wire," you need a third wire for ground reference. Without a common ground, the common-mode voltage between master and slave can exceed the receiver's Â±7V tolerance range, especially when devices are on different power supplies.

  • Connect the ground/common terminals of all devices on the bus
  • The cable shield should be grounded at one end only to prevent ground loops
  • If devices are in different panels with different earth references, use an isolating RS-485 repeater
